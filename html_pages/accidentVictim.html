What to do if you are involved in a Car Accident



Accident scene

Please follow these steps to ensure less lives are lost if possible

  1. Check yourself to see if you are hurt:

    First check ypurself to know if you have been injured whether minor or majorly. Try to access how well you can move your limbs. Remember you have to be fit to help others.

  2. Check the other person or people for Injuries:

    If any other person(s) are injured, first access the extent of injury(ies). Treat the quietest person first, they are usually the most fatal. For people who can scream or talk, you can ask of their names, if he/she responds, then you know they can access the situation and has most likely not suffered severe head injury.

  3. Look for signs of breathing:

    Next, check if the person is breathing and if he has a pulse.

  4. Call for help:

    Immediately call for an ambulance or rush the person to a hospital.

  5. Check for obstructions in the personâ€™s mouth and throat:

    If you do not hear any breath sounds, check his/her mouth for any obstructions. If there is something obstructing the airway, use your index and middle finger to clear the airway.

  6. Perform life saving techniques:

    If there is no pulse, start CPR. Keep the person s neck straight to start CPR (Cardio pulmonary resuscitation).You could read more about how to perform CPR here.

  7. Ways to help him/her in grave situations:

    If there is bleeding from the mouth or the patient is vomiting, turn the person to his/her side. This will avoid any chances of the person choking. Place the person's arm that is under him/her straight out and the arm closest to you across his chest.

  8. Keep the person warm:

    Usually accident victims feel excessively cold due to shock and trauma. Therefore keeping them warm is essential to survival. You can use whatever you have to do this, such as a T-shirt, jacket, etc.

  9. Avoid feeding the person:

    Do not give the person any water, food or other fluids through the mouth, it could lead to the patient choking.
